The Knock at the Door: Understanding the Panic Button

ID: 009 // CLEARANCE: BLACK_BOX

Milliseconds Matter

When security is breached—whether it's a physical raid, a network intrusion, or a theft in progress—you do not have time to navigate menus. You do not have time to click "Safely Eject Hardware." You need the data gone, instantly.

The Kill Switch Logic

HoloSec features a global hotkey (The Panic Button). When triggered, the engine executes a three-stage sequence in under 200 milliseconds:

  1. Unmount: The encrypted volume is forcibly detached from the operating system.
  2. RAM Flush: The encryption keys, which live only in Volatile RAM, are overwritten with zeros.
  3. App Termination: The HoloSec process terminates itself.

Cold Boot Attack Prevention

Because the keys are flushed from RAM instantly, even a sophisticated "Cold Boot Attack" (freezing the RAM chips to read data after power-off) is mitigated. By the time anyone touches the keyboard, the data is encrypted, the keys are vaporized, and the computer looks like a clean slate.
