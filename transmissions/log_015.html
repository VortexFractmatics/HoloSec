Your Tax Returns Are a Goldmine for Identity Thieves

ID: 015 // CLEARANCE: BLACK_BOX

The PDF Problem

Most people keep their tax returns (1040s, W-2s) in a folder named "Taxes" on their desktop. This is the first place malware looks. A single PDF of your tax return contains your SSN, address, income, and employer data. It is a complete Identity Theft kit.

The Vault Strategy

HoloSec recommends a "Cold Storage" approach for financial documents. You create a dedicated vault specifically for archival data. This vault is encrypted with AES-256 + Twofish (Cascade Encryption).

Why not use a password-protected PDF?

Adobe's built-in password protection is notoriously weak and can be cracked in minutes with GPU acceleration. HoloSec encrypts the volume, not the file. This means the file structure itself is hidden. A hacker cannot even see that you have a tax return, let alone try to crack it.
