"Quishing": The Danger of Random QR Codes

ID: 048 // CLEARANCE: BLACK_BOX

The Parking Meter Scam

You see a QR code sticker on a parking meter that says "Pay Here." You scan it. You enter your credit card. You just handed your data to a scammer who pasted that sticker over the real one. This is "Quishing" (QR Phishing).

Malicious Redirects

QR codes are just URLs. They can lead to malware downloads or fake login pages. Because you cannot read the URL before you scan, you are trusting the code blindly.

The Safe Scanner

Never scan a QR code with your primary device if you can avoid it. And never, ever store your QR-based 2FA backup codes in a cloud folder. Use HoloSec to store images of your backup codes offline, where they cannot be scraped by automated bots looking for that distinctive square pattern.
