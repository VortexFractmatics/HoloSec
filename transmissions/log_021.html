The Lockout

You click a link. Suddenly, your screen turns red. "Your files have been encrypted. Pay 2 BTC to unlock them." Ransomware doesn't care if you are a Fortune 500 company or a student. It encrypts everything it can touch: your C: drive, your connected backup drive, your Dropbox folder.

The Immune Vault

HoloSec archives are immune to standard ransomware for two reasons:

  1. The Container Format: Ransomware scripts look for .docx, .jpg, and .pdf files. They often skip undefined, large binary blobs (which is what a HoloSec volume looks like).
  2. The Offline State: If your critical backups are stored in a HoloSec vault on a disconnected drive, the ransomware cannot reach them. When the attack happens, you simply wipe your PC, reinstall Windows, and plug in your HoloSec drive. You are back in business in 30 minutes, zero dollars paid.
