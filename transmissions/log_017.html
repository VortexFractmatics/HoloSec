Crossing Borders: The "Clean" Laptop Strategy

ID: 017 // CLEARANCE: BLACK_BOX

The Customs Checkpoint Danger

You land in a foreign country. The customs agent asks you to unlock your laptop. Legally, in many jurisdictions, you must comply or face deportation/arrest. If you unlock it, they can copy your hard drive in minutes.

The "Burner" Profile

HoloSec advocates a strict travel protocol. Your laptop should be "Clean." It should contain nothing but a fresh installation of Windows and maybe a few innocent movies or games.

The Invisible Cargo

Your actual data lives on a HoloSec encrypted microSD card or a tiny USB drive hidden on your person (or encrypted in the cloud using our Shard protocol). If the agent searches the laptop, they find nothing. It looks like a boring, standard computer.

Even if they find the USB drive and force you to plug it in, you enter your Duress Code (see Log 002). The drive opens, revealing... nothing but a collection of public domain e-books. The sensitive data is hidden in the volume's free space, mathematically undetectable. You cross the border with your secrets intact.
