The Risk of Inaction

Most security systems fail when the user cannot act. If you are incapacitated, arrested, or worse, your laptop sits in an evidence locker. Time is on the attacker's side. They have infinite time to brute-force your key.

The Countdown Timer

HoloSec inverts this dynamic. You configure a "Heartbeat Interval" (e.g., 7 Days). Every time you open your vault, the heartbeat resets. If the timer hits 00:00:00, the system assumes the user is compromised.

The Shredding Sequence (Gutmann Method)

HoloSec does not simply "delete" the file (which is recoverable). It initiates a forensic scrub of the Volume Header:

  • Pass 1-4: Overwrite with random pseudo-noise.
  • Pass 5-20: Overwrite with specific bit patterns (to defeat magnetic resonance microscopy).
  • Pass 21-35: Overwrite with zeros.

Once the header is destroyed, the encrypted data on the drive becomes "orphaned." It has no map. It is indistinguishable from empty disk space. Even if you were to return on Day 8 and type the correct password, the vault would not open. It no longer exists.
