The Flaw of Standard Encryption

If you encrypt a file with standard tools (like 7-Zip AES or basic PGP), you create a "Black Box." It is locked, yes, but it looks like a locked box. A forensic analyst looking at your drive sees a block of data that is clearly encrypted. This is called "High Entropy Data." It screams to the investigator: "Here are the secrets. Start cracking."

Obfuscation vs. Encryption
