The Trojan Horse: Supply Chain Attacks

ID: 025 // CLEARANCE: BLACK_BOX

Trust No One

In 2020, SolarWinds—a major IT software vendor—pushed a legitimate update to thousands of companies. That update contained a Russian backdoor. This is a Supply Chain Attack. You didn't download a virus; you downloaded a trusted update from a trusted vendor, and you still got hacked.

Updates are Liabilities

Every piece of software that "phones home" for updates is a potential vector. If the vendor gets hacked, you get hacked.

The Frozen Core

HoloSec is designed as "Static Architecture." Once you download the engine, it does not auto-update. It does not phone home. It is a frozen, immutable block of code. This means that even if we were compromised tomorrow (which is impossible due to our offline key generation), your existing copy of HoloSec would remain secure. You control the update cycle, not us.
