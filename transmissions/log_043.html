Your Car is Selling Your Driving Data

ID: 043 // CLEARANCE: BLACK_BOX

The Insurance Betrayal

Modern cars are smartphones on wheels. Manufacturers like GM, Honda, and Hyundai track your hard braking, rapid acceleration, and late-night driving. They sell this data to data brokers like LexisNexis, who then sell it to insurance companies. Your rates go up because you braked too hard on Tuesday.

No Opt-Out

Often, this consent is buried in the "Connected Services" terms you clicked on the dashboard. You cannot easily opt out without losing navigation features.

Data Separation

While HoloSec cannot stop your car from spying, it enforces the philosophy of "Compartmentalization." Never connect your primary phone (with your HoloSec vault keys) to the car's Bluetooth or USB port. Using the car's infotainment system gives the manufacturer access to your contacts and messages. Keep your digital life air-gapped from your vehicle.
