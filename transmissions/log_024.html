Why VPNs Are Not a Silver Bullet

ID: 024 // CLEARANCE: BLACK_BOX

The Coffee Shop Trap

You sit down at Starbucks. You connect to "Starbucks_Guest." But is it really Starbucks? Or is it a "Pineapple" device sitting in a hacker's backpack at the next table, intercepting all your traffic? This is a Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attack.

"But I use a VPN!"

A VPN encrypts your traffic tunnel, yes. But if the hacker controls the access point, they can attempt to strip the SSL encryption or inject malware into the connection handshake before the VPN fully engages. Or, your VPN might drop for a split second (leak), exposing you.

The Air-Gap Solution

The only way to be 100% safe on public Wi-Fi is to ensure your critical data never touches the Wi-Fi card. With HoloSec, you work out of an encrypted container that has no network permissions. Even if the hacker owns the network, they cannot see inside a vault that is mathematically sealed.
