How to Smuggle Data in a JPEG

ID: 005 // CLEARANCE: BLACK_BOX

Why encrypting a file isn't enough

If you email a file named TOP_SECRET.docx.aes, you have flagged yourself. Even if they can't read it, they know it's valuable. They will block the email or detain you.

The Art of LSB Injection

HoloSec uses a technique called Least Significant Bit (LSB) Steganography. Digital images are made of pixels. Each pixel has color values (Red, Green, Blue). If you change the color value by 1/255th, the human eye literally cannot see the difference.

HoloSec takes your sensitive text file (seed phrases, blueprints, contacts) and breaks it down into bits. It then scatters those bits into the invisible color variations of a high-resolution image (like a photo of your dog or a landscape).

The Result

You have a valid .JPG file. It opens in Windows Photo Viewer. It opens in Photoshop. It looks normal. But it carries a payload. You can upload it to Flickr, email it to your mom, or put it on a USB drive. It bypasses Deep Packet Inspection (DPI) because mathematically, it is just an image.
