The Scenario: You are crossing a border. Or perhaps you are in a hostile environment. You are detained. The agent does not care about your rights. They place your laptop on the table and say, "Unlock it."

This is the "Rubber Hose Attack." Standard encryption (AES-256) is mathematically unbreakable, but human will is weak. If you refuse, you suffer. If you comply, you compromise your entire network. With BitLocker or VeraCrypt, you have no third option.

Enter the "Duress" State

HoloSec changes the geometry of the situation. When you initialize your vault, you create two realities:

  1. Reality A (The Vault): Your actual data. Blueprints. Seed phrases. Source lists.
  2. Reality B (The Decoy): A fully functional "fake" vault.

If you type your Master Password, Reality A opens. But if you type your Duress Password, the software behaves exactly the same wayâ€”it mounts a drive letter, decrypts files, and shows a folder structure. But that folder is filled with pre-loaded "innocent" data (family photos, tax returns, non-sensitive work).

The attacker sees the drive unlock. They see files. They believe they have broken you. You are released. Your actual data remains hidden in the mathematical "void" of the drive, completely untouched and undetectable.
